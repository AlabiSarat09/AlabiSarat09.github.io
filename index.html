Global Education Inequality Dashboard

Education access data across 42,187 students from multiple countries. The question was simple: who graduates, and who makes that call. The answer split cleanly along three lines — gender, location, and wealth.

  • Sub-Saharan Africa held 7,900+ students in the poorest bracket against 2,300 in the richest — the widest gap of any region studied
  • Urban students outpaced rural peers in completion at every education level
  • Gender dominance in completion rates flipped depending on the country — no single global pattern held

Customer Retention Analysis

2,594 customer records, one question: why are people leaving, and can we see it coming. Three factors explained almost two-thirds of all churn.

  • Short-term contracts, fiber-optic usage, and payment method together accounted for 65%+ of churn cases
  • 7,043 customers flagged as at-risk at a 41.9% churn rate
  • Modeled scenario: 310 customers retained, 12% churn reduction, ₦328,000 in annual revenue preserved

Employee Performance Analysis

A dataset shared during a formal job shadowing program at Interswitch. The most interesting finding wasn't where performance was highest — it was where pay and performance disagreed with each other.

  • Lagos contributed more than 60% of total performance output across the company
  • Employees with 5–9 years of tenure held the highest performance share at 32.3% — a possible retention-worthy window
  • Legal and Capability Development departments showed high salaries against low performance ratings
